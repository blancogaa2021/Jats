title: Weight Gain
description: "The effect of overeating on weight gain: A one-sample t-test."
source: "Moore, D.S., McCabe, G.P., Craig, B.A. (2012). Introduction to the Practice of Statistics (7th ed.). New York, NY: W.H. Freeman and Company, Chapter 7, p. 424; from Levine, J.A., Eberhardt, N.L., & Jensen, M.D. (1999). Role of nonexcercise activity thermogenesis in resistance to fat gain in humans. Science, 283, 212-214. Data are available at www.sciencemag.org"
hypothesis of interest: The average weight gain after 8-weeks of overeating (a total of 56,000 extra calories) does not equal 16 pounds (lb); Test value = 16.

tags:
  - OneSampleTtest

variables:
  - name: Weight Before 
    type: Continuous
    description: Weight in pounds (lb) measured before 8-weeks of overeating
    
  - name: Weight After 
    type: Continuous
    description: Weight in pounds (lb) measured after 8-weeks of overeating
 
  - name: Difference 
    type: Continuous
    description: Weight Before - Weight After
